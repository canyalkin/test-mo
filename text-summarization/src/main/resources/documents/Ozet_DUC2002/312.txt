hurricane hugo struck south carolina thursday after thousands of coastal residents were evacuated inland.
by late afternoon the storm was upgraded to category 4.
hurricane-force winds first hit the coast at 9:15 p.m. and by midnight 119 mph winds were reported in charleston and up to 135 mph as the eye passed nearby.
hugo was expected to turn north and cause severe flooding as it passed up the coast.
as of thursday evening about 12,000 people had checked into 83 shelters across south carolina.
a spokesman for the red cross said that tens of thousands of people were in shelters along the eastern seaboard.
