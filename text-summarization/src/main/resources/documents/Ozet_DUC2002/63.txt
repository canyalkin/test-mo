the bundesbank, germany's independent central bank, is resisting political pressure from the german government and the governments and central banks of europe to cut interest rates in order to ease large german deficits and inflationary pressures.
the european currency bloc is overvalued against the dollar and yen as a result of the bundesbank's policies.
very high real interest rates threaten to turn a european recession into a depression.
european governments believe the bundesbank's intransigence is threatening nearly 10 years of effort for greater european economic and political integration and urge it to become more responsive to broader european interests.
