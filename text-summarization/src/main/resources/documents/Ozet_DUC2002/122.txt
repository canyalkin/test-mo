british prime minister margaret thatcher and soviet president mikhail gorbachev emerged from a meeting in which they were said to have argued passionately.
a thatcher spokesman said the soviet president and the prime minister loved arguing.
subjects covered in the meeting ranged from nuclear disarmament to the sale of soviet bombers to libya.
when thatcher asked gorbachev about the sale, the soviet president did not respond.
thatcher expressed doubts on the soviet statement about the soviet's chemical arsenal and disagreed with gorbachev's position that nuclear weapons should be eliminated from europe.
mrs. thatcher believes that the presence of nuclear weapons deters war.
