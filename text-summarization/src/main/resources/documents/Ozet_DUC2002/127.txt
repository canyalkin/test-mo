a major earthquake lasting about fifteen seconds and measuring 6.5 on the richter scale, rocked northern california on tuesday evening.
one section of the san francisco bay bridge collapsed.
nearby buildings swayed several feet and some may have collapsed.
phones and power were knocked out.
fans at candlestick park waiting for the world series to begin screamed as the stadium swayed for about fifteen seconds.
fans were evacuated and there were reports of some injuries.
the quake shook buildings in sacramento, 95 miles to the northeast, and was felt as far away as reno, nv, 225 miles to the east.
